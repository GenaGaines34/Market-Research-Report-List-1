<p><p>The nonwoven disposable gloves market includes several manufacturing types: </p><p>1. **Spunlace/Spunbond**: Fabrics are created by entangling fibers with high-pressure water jets or mechanical means, offering flexibility and strength.</p><p>   </p><p>2. **Wetlaid**: This method uses a slurry of fibers on a substrate, producing smooth, uniform sheets ideal for medical use.</p><p>3. **Airlaid**: Fibers are laid down in a web using air currents, creating absorbent materials.</p><p>4. **Needle Punched**: Fibers are densely bonded through needle penetration, providing durability.</p><p>5. **Others**: Includes various proprietary or hybrid techniques, expanding diverse applications.</p></p>
