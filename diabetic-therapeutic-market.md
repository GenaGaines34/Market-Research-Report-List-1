<p><p>The diabetic therapeutic market encompasses various classes of drugs designed to manage blood glucose levels in diabetes. Sulphonylureas stimulate insulin secretion; Biguanides, like metformin, improve insulin sensitivity and reduce liver glucose production. Meglitinides also promote insulin release but with shorter action. Thiazolidinediones enhance insulin sensitivity in tissues. Alpha-glucosidase inhibitors slow carbohydrate absorption, while DPP-4 inhibitors increase incretin levels, promoting insulin release. SGLT-2 inhibitors reduce glucose reabsorption in kidneys, leading to increased glucose excretion. Each class offers unique benefits and mechanisms for diabetes management.</p></p>
