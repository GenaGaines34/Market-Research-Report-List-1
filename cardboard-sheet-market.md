<p><p>The cardboard sheet market can be broadly categorized into two types: native paper and recycling paper. Native paper refers to sheets made from virgin fibers, offering superior strength and quality, ideal for high-end packaging applications. Conversely, recycling paper consists of materials repurposed from used cardboard, making it a more sustainable and cost-effective option. While native paper is preferred for its durability and aesthetics, recycling paper caters to environmentally conscious consumers and businesses aiming to minimize waste and reduce their carbon footprint.</p></p>
