<p><p>The medical device pneumatic cylinders market is segmented into single-acting cylinders, double-acting cylinders, and others. Single-acting cylinders use air pressure to move in one direction, relying on gravity or a spring for return, making them simple and cost-effective. In contrast, double-acting cylinders utilize air pressure for both extension and retraction, providing greater control and force, ideal for more complex applications. "Others" may include specialty cylinders designed for unique medical applications, incorporating advanced technologies and custom features.</p></p>
