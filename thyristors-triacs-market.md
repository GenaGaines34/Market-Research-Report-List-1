Thyristors, primarily used in high-power applications, allow current to flow in one direction and can be turned on by a gate signal. <p><p>The thyristor market encompasses several types, including Silicon Controlled Rectifiers (SCRs), Gate Turn-Off Thyristors (GTOs), and Integrated Gate-Commutated Thyristors (IGCTs), along with others. SCRs are widely used for controlling power in various applications, while GTOs allow for external gate control for turning off. IGCTs combine the advantages of both SCRs and GTOs, offering faster switching capabilities and higher efficiency. Additional types include light-activated and bidirectional thyristors, catering to diverse industrial and consumer electronics applications.</p></p>
