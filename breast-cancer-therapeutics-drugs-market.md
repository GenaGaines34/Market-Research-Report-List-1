<p><p>The breast cancer therapeutics drug market comprises several key types. Mitotic inhibitors, such as taxanes, disrupt cell division, while anti-metabolites interfere with DNA synthesis. Hormone receptor therapies target estrogen and progesterone receptors to stop cancer growth. Aromatase inhibitors reduce estrogen production in postmenopausal women, slowing tumor progression. HER2 inhibitors, like trastuzumab, specifically target the HER2 protein overexpressed in some breast cancers, enhancing treatment effectiveness. Together, these categories address diverse breast cancer subtypes, improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
