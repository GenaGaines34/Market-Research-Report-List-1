<p><p>Digital Mapping Cameras (DMC) are specialized devices used for capturing high-resolution geospatial imagery for mapping and surveying purposes. The market is mainly segmented into Linear Array Scanners (Pushbroom) and Area Arrays. Linear Array Scanners capture images in a continuous line as the sensor moves, ensuring high detail over large areas, typically used in aerial mapping. Conversely, Area Arrays capture images in a matrix format all at once, providing extensive coverage in a single snapshot, often utilized in urban planning and infrastructure projects.</p></p>
