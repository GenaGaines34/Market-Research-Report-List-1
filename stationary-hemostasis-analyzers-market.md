<p><p>Stationary hemostasis analyzers are crucial in diagnosing coagulation disorders by assessing blood clotting. The market is primarily divided into two types: optical detection and mechanical detection. </p><p>Optical detection analyzers use light-based technologies to measure changes in light transmission or scattering as blood coagulates, providing accurate and real-time results. In contrast, mechanical detection analyzers utilize physical methods, such as the measurement of pressure or movement, to assess clot formation and stability. Both technologies contribute significantly to patient care and treatment decisions.</p></p>
