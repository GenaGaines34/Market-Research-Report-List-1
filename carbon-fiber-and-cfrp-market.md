<p><p>Carbon fiber is a lightweight, high-strength material used in various industries, notably aerospace, automotive, and sports equipment. The carbon fiber reinforced polymer (CFRP) market can be categorized into two types: thermosetting and thermoplastic CFRP. Thermosetting CFRP is created by curing a resin, resulting in rigid, durable composites ideal for high-performance applications. In contrast, thermoplastic CFRP utilizes heat to mold the polymer, allowing for easier recycling and fabrication, making it suitable for applications requiring flexibility and toughness.</p></p>
