<p><p>The golf shoes market is primarily divided into two types: spiked and spikeless. Spiked golf shoes feature metal or plastic cleats that provide superior traction on the course, making them ideal for wet or uneven terrain. Conversely, spikeless golf shoes have a flat sole with rubber traction patterns, offering versatility and comfort for both on and off the course. While spiked shoes prioritize stability, spikeless options emphasize convenience and style, appealing to a broader range of golfers.</p></p>
