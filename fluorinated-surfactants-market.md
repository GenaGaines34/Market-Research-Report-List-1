Ionic fluorinated surfactants, which include anions or cations, are known for their strong surface activity and are often used in applications like coatings and cleaning products. Non-ionic fluorinated surfactants, lacking charge, offer versatility and stability in diverse environments, making them suitable for personal care and industrial formulations. Both types provide enhanced properties such as low surface tension and water repellency, catering to various applications across industries.</p></p>
