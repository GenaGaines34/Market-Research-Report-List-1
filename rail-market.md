<p><p>Rail market types can be categorized into heavy rail and light rail. Heavy rail systems, like subways and commuter trains, are designed for high-capacity urban transit, operating on dedicated tracks at higher speeds and with greater frequency. Light rail, on the other hand, operates on lighter vehicles and often shares roadways with other traffic, suitable for shorter distances. These systems enhance urban mobility, reduce congestion, and provide eco-friendly transportation options tailored to the varying needs of urban and suburban areas.</p></p>
