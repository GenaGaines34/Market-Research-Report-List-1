<p><p>The Pulsed Field Gel Electrophoresis (PFGE) market encompasses various techniques used for separating large DNA and protein molecules. Key types include Rotating Gel Electrophoresis (RGE), which employs rotational fields; Field-Inversion Gel Electrophoresis (FIGE), utilizing alternating field directions; Transverse-Alternating Field Gel Electrophoresis (TAFE) for enhanced resolution; and Contour-Clamped Homogeneous Electric Fields (CHEF), which provides uniformity. Orthogonal-Field Alternation Gel Electrophoresis (OFAGE) introduces varied field angles, while Programmable Autonomously-Controlled Electrodes (PACE) uses adaptive controls. Pulsed-Homogeneous Orthogonal Field Gel Electrophoresis (PHOGE) optimizes electrophoretic conditions for superior DNA separation.</p></p>
