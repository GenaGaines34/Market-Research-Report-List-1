<p><p>The bradycardia drugs market includes several key medications used to manage slow heart rates. Atropine acts as an anticholinergic, increasing heart rate by blocking vagal effects. Isoproterenol is a sympathomimetic agent that stimulates beta-adrenergic receptors, effectively raising heart rates. Aminophylline, primarily a bronchodilator, can also enhance heart function. Ephedrine, a stimulant, raises heart rates and blood pressure. Scopolamine, while primarily used for motion sickness, may occasionally influence heart rate through its anticholinergic effects.</p></p>
