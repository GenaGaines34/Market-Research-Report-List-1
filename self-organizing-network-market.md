<p><strong>The Self-Organizing Network Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>D-SON</li><li>C-SON</li><li>Hybrid SON</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Self-Organizing Networks (SON) enhance network management through automation. D-SON (Distributed SON) operates at the local level, optimizing network elements independently. C-SON (Centralized SON) centralizes decision-making, allowing for unified management across multiple network nodes, improving coordination and efficiency. Hybrid SON combines both approaches, leveraging centralized oversight while allowing for localized adjustments, optimizing performance and resource allocation. These market types cater to diverse network management needs, balancing autonomy and control for improved operational efficiency in telecom environments.</p></p>
