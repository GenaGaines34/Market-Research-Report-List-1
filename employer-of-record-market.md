<p><p>An Employer of Record (EOR) is a service model that allows companies to outsource their employee management functions. The EOR takes on the legal responsibilities of an employer, handling payroll, tax compliance, benefits administration, and other HR functions, while the client company retains control over day-to-day operations and employee work. This model is especially appealing to businesses expanding into new markets, as it mitigates compliance risks and simplifies the hiring process.</p><p>The Employer of Record Market is anticipated to grow at a CAGR of 6.80% during the forecast period. Key trends driving this growth include an increase in remote work, globalization of the workforce, and a rise in the gig economy. Companies are increasingly relying on EOR services to access talent without the complexities of establishing a legal entity in different jurisdictions. Additionally, the heightened focus on compliance and risk management is prompting organizations to seek specialized EOR services. As businesses look for flexibility and efficiency in their workforce management, the demand for EOR solutions is likely to increase, solidifying its position in the HR outsourcing landscape.</p></p>

<p><p>The Employer of Record (EOR) market can be categorized into two main types: the Aggregator Model and the Wholly Owned Infrastructure Model. </p><p>In the Aggregator Model, multiple EOR providers collaborate to offer services, allowing for flexibility and cost-sharing without heavy infrastructure investments. Conversely, the Wholly Owned Infrastructure Model involves a single EOR managing all aspects of employment services independently, granting greater control and consistency in compliance and operations. Each model serves different business needs and market dynamics, influencing how companies manage HR functions.</p></p>
