<p><p>The carbon dioxide gas sensor market features various types, including NDIR (Non-Dispersive Infrared) sensors, which detect CO2 levels by measuring infrared light absorption. Catalytic CO2 sensors utilize chemical reactions to identify gas concentration, often found in industrial settings. Thermal conductivity sensors measure changes in heat transfer caused by CO2 presence, useful for safety applications. Each sensor type serves distinct applications, with NDIR being most common for environmental monitoring, while thermal conductivity and catalytic sensors are more specialized for industrial needs.</p></p>
